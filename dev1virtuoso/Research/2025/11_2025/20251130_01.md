## Executive Summary  
Heracles is a completely open-source humanoid robotics research platform that has evolved from 2016–2025 chased the absolute maximum possible degrees of freedom and human-like flexibility, and from 2025–2026 pivoted to the absolute minimum cost while still satisfying three non-negotiable physical demonstrations:

1. Single leg straight raise above head (hip flexion ≥ 155°, foot touches head)  
2. Full 180°–190° front split (一字馬)  
3. 90°+ backbend bridge (腰部後折 90°–95°)

The project now contains five fully documented hardware generations. Physical construction resumes in 2026 with V5 targeting a complete, walking, dancing, back-bending robot for **HKD 5,800–6,000** (≈ USD 740–770) using only off-the-shelf 2026 parts.

## Critical Torque Reality Check (All Versions)

| Motion | Required Peak Torque | Bare 1.2 N·m | 5:1 Planetary (V5) | 10:1 Planetary (V3–V4) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Single-leg stance (25 kg) | 18–25 N·m hip | Collapse | OK (7–8 N·m + momentum) | Comfortable |
| Jump / fast squat | 30–45 N·m knee | Impossible | Marginal (needs skill) | Easy |
| Leg raise to head | 15–18 N·m hip | Impossible | Achieved | Easy |
| 90° backbend (5 segments) | 6–9 N·m per segment | Overheat | Achieved | Easy |

→ All versions after V1 **must** use planetary geared steppers. V5 uses the new 2026 ultra-cheap 5:1 series.

## Software & Control (unchanged across all versions)

- RL: PyTorch + PPO  
- Primary simulator: MuJoCo (fastest iteration)  
- Secondary: Gazebo + ROS 2  
- Motion decomposition: micro-operations  
- Generative pipeline: BERT → Transformer dance/music  
- Dataset: S.O.F.I.A. (video + per-frame JSON)

## Final Conclusion & Roadmap

- 2016–2024: Pure theory & maximum DOF (V1–V2)  
- 2025: Theoretical peak (V3) → realistic but still expensive (V4)  
- 2026: Heracles V5: the first version that an individual can actually build for **under HKD 6,000** while still performing leg-raise-to-head, full split, and 95° backbend.

All CAD, firmware, RL code, MuJoCo models will be released open-source on GitHub.

The dream of a truly flexible, dancing, back-bending humanoid robot for less than the price of a high-end electric scooter is now mechanically and financially possible.
