# Modified Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform V6

The Modified Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform V6 builds upon the original V6 conceptual design, evolving it into a more practical, distributed-control system while maintaining the core goals of high dexterity, extreme flexibility (including 155°+ leg raise, 180°–190° front splits, and 95° backbend), and cost-effectiveness for open-source research.

Key upgrades include:
- Distributed control: 2× Raspberry Pi + 13× Arduino Mega 2560 managing 66 motors via DRV8825 drivers.
- Enhanced hand/foot dexterity: 20 micro motors for fingers + 10 for toes (independent control using YYZD-5K L547 equivalents).
- Maintenance-friendly structure: Exposed RPi/Arduino/DRV8825 clusters with removable screw-fixed cover panels (M3) on chest, back, pelvis, side waist, thighs, and arms.
- Waist optimization: 5-segment spine (10 motors for pitch + roll) with 360° turret (timing belt 1:3 reduction); no shrimp-shell joints for bidirectional bending.
- Actuation mix: Planetary geared steppers for major joints + micro lead-screw steppers for extremities.

Total: ~89 DOF with 66 motors. Estimated BOM remains in the low-cost range (~USD 1,500–1,800 projected for 2026), making full human-like performance accessible to individual builders.

## Design Philosophy and Key Modifications

The original V5/V6 focused on ultra-low cost (under HKD 6,000 for V5) while achieving three non-negotiable demonstrations:
1. Single-leg straight raise above head (hip flexion ≥155°).
2. Full 180°–190° front split.
3. 90°–95° backbend bridge.

Modifications in V6 prioritize:
- Dexterity: Independent finger/toe control for fine manipulation (grip bottle, OK gesture, typing, middle finger).
- Serviceability: Half-exposed electronics with easy-access panels.
- Reliability: RS485 bus for robust multi-node communication; DRV8825 drivers for all 66 motors.
- Realism: Waist uses segmented design (forward/backward bending to 95° cumulative) without unidirectional "shrimp-shell" joints; elbows/knees retain one-way rigid support with flexible membrane lining.

All CAD, firmware, MuJoCo models, and RL code (PyTorch + PPO) will be open-sourced on GitHub.

## Control Architecture

- Master Controller: Raspberry Pi 1 (main) — sends high-level commands to all Arduinos.
- Auxiliary: Raspberry Pi 2 (support tasks, e.g., vision/IMU fusion).
- Distributed Nodes: 13× Arduino Mega 2560, grouped as follows:

| Arduino ID | Responsible Regions | Motors Controlled |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | Neck + Shoulders | Neck 3 + Shoulders 4 (2 per side) |
| A2 | Elbows + Wrists | Elbows 2 (1 per side) + Wrists 2 (1 per side) |
| A3 | Left hand fingers (front half) | 5 micro motors |
| A4 | Left hand fingers (rear half) | 5 micro motors |
| A5 | Right hand fingers (front half) | 5 micro motors |
| A6 | Right hand fingers (rear half) | 5 micro motors |
| A7 | Waist front segments + Turret | Waist front 3 segments (6 motors) + Turret 1 |
| A8 | Waist rear segments | Waist rear 2 segments (4 motors) |
| A9 | Left lower limb major joints | Left hip 4 + Left knee 1 + Left ankle 3 |
| A10 | Left ankle + partial toes | Left ankle 3 + Left toes front 3 |
| A11 | Right lower limb major joints | Right hip 4 + Right knee 1 + Right ankle 3 |
| A12 | Right ankle + partial toes | Right ankle 3 + Right toes front 3 |
| A13 | Remaining toes | Left toes rear 2 + Right toes rear 2 |

Electronics Placement: RPi, Arduinos, and DRV8825 drivers are mounted for ~50% external exposure (visible clusters for debugging/monitoring) while protected by removable panels.

Power: 2× 2S 7.4v LiPo batteries.

Extremities: 30× micro lead-screw steppers (6mm class, YYZD-5K L547 equivalents) for fingers/toes.

Drivers: 66× DRV8825 (chosen for compact size, high microstepping, and suitability for mixed current needs; alternative TB6600/HY-DIV268N possible for higher-power joints).

## Maintenance and Structural Design

- Exposed Electronics: RPi boards, Arduino Megas, and DRV8825 drivers mounted with approximately half their volume externally visible for quick diagnostics, LED monitoring, and firmware updates.
- Access Panels: Removable cover plates on:
  - Chest
  - Back
  - Pelvis
  - Side waist
  - Thighs
  - Arms
- Fixing: M3 screws for all panels — tool-friendly and vibration-resistant.
- Materials: 6061 aluminum CNC + carbon fiber plates for structural rigidity and low weight.

## Tendon and Joint Mechanism Details

- Tendons: 1 mm Dyneema + springs + pulleys (optimized routing for micro motors).
- Waist: No shrimp-shell (unidirectional) joints — full bidirectional bending (forward/backward to 95° cumulative) using segmented motors. Yaw handled by turret + whole-body coordination.
- Elbows & Knees: One-directional bending with rigid external support; flexible membrane inner lining connected via embedded grooves or through-holes.
- Neck & Wrists: Multi-directional tendon drive — no one-way joints required.
- Micro Actuators: Lead-screw type for precise, compact finger/toe control.

## Communication Protocol

- Bus: RS485 half-duplex on single twisted-pair (A/B lines + GND).
- Transceiver: MAX485 modules on each Arduino (TTL to RS485 conversion).
- Topology: All 13 Arduinos paralleled on one bus; 120Ω termination resistors at both ends.
- Master-Slave: RPi 1 broadcasts commands; Arduinos handle local PID/step generation and feedback.
- Software: Custom protocol over RS485; compatible with MuJoCo simulation for transfer.

## Conclusion

The Modified Heracles V6 achieves a compelling balance of extreme flexibility, fine dexterity, and practical buildability. With distributed Arduino nodes, DRV8825 drivers, exposed-yet-protected electronics, and optimized waist design, it pushes the boundary of what an individual or small team can construct for under USD 2,000 while delivering human-like performance in key demonstrations.

This open-source platform continues the Heracles vision: a fully flexible, dancing, back-bending humanoid that democratizes advanced robotics research.

All files (CAD, code, BOM spreadsheets, MuJoCo XML) will be released publicly.
