# Modified Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform V6

## Summary and Strategic Discontinuation

This technical report delineates the final architectural status and total project suspension parameters of the Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform V6 (Modified 20260521). Building upon the technical insights and milestones gained from prior iterations[^1], this release documents the complete and permanent cessation of all development tracks, including physical hardware manufacturing and digital simulation workflows.

The driving rationale behind this transition is the immediate freezing of development across all domains. This formal lock establishes the state of proposed hardware configurations, network topologies, common-ground safety protocols, and software control mechanisms, rendering them purely archival assets. All engineering operations, empirical modeling pipelines, active tuning tasks, physical prototyping, mechanical assembly, and virtual environment simulation testing have been completely suspended.[^9]

## Background and Project Lifecycle Context

Project Heracles (originally conceptualized as Project Mojave) was initiated to transcend the classical limitations of humanoid degrees of freedom (DOF) and dexterous manipulation[^3]. The platform evolved progressively through several distinct design phases:

* **V1 and V2:** Purely theoretical frameworks focused on maximum possible DOFs utilizing zero-compromise antagonistic dual-motor actuators[^1].
* **V3:** The absolute theoretical peak, implementing a 141-motor setup that proved highly descriptive but financially prohibitive[^1][^2].
* **V4:** A realistic, 73-motor spring-return hybrid configuration designed to minimize costs by substituting active actuators with passive springs[^1][^2].
* **V5:** The first buildable real-world iteration achieved in 2025, reducing the actuator count to 46 motors to bring the physical construction budget under HKD 6,000 while maintaining extreme flexibility, such as full splits and a 95 degree backbend[^2].

The baseline V6 architecture was originally targeted to introduce human-like hand and foot dexterity coupled with a multi-segment Bionic Shrimp-Shell Joint structure[^5]. This bionic spine/limb design utilizes a rigid-soft coupled mechanism operating via non-linear, non-conjugate surfaces (Reverse Conjugate Surface, or RCS Geometry) to withstand high impacts and distribute strain[^5]. Physical prototyping was previously halted to avoid mechanical tolerances, tendon wear on the Dyneema routing links, and micro-motor back-EMF feedback. Following the comprehensive closure directive, the subsequent track to execute zero-shot stability and reinforcement learning optimization within virtual physics environments has also been suspended. The Modified V6 framework now stands frozen as a historical reference model.

## Electromechanical Wiring and Network Topology Specifications

### High-Power Actuator Drive Loop

The primary locomotion and structural joints were mapped around GIM6010-8 24V star-winding joint motors paired with GDS68 drivers. The high-current power distribution loop parameters are recorded as follows:

* **Positive Terminal (+):** The 24V power supply positive rail was routed to the motor driver's positive power input (labeled as V+ or VIN).
* **Negative Terminal (-):** The 24V power supply negative rail was routed to the driver's power ground (GND).
* **Conductor Specification:** Interconnections utilized XT60 2+2 heavy-duty lines to mitigate instantaneous voltage sag under peak structural load conditions.

### Low-Power Logic and Communication Protocol

The control topology was designed to isolate logic signaling from high-power current loops to prevent computational instability:

* **Microcontroller Supply:** The 13 distributed Arduino Mega 2560 nodes were configured to receive logic power independently via isolated 5V/9V power adapters through their onboard USB/DC ports, blocking high-power inductive motor spikes.
* **CAN Bus Infrastructure:** For decentralized control communication, each Arduino Mega 2560 was configured to interface with an MCP2515 CAN module via the SPI bus. The communication lines were established by daisy-chaining the module's CAN_H and CAN_L terminals to the respective CAN_H and CAN_L pins on the GDS68 motor drivers. The network was targeted to operate at a high-speed baud rate of 1 Mbps to ensure low transmission latency ($\tau < 20 \text{ ms}$)[^8].

### Critical Safety Protocol: Common Ground Alignment

To mitigate ground-potential drifting caused by the rapid high-frequency switching of the joint motors—which distorts CAN bus differential signaling and risks damaging low-voltage ICs—the architecture required a mandatory Common Ground configuration:

> **Mandatory Action:** A dedicated ground wire was specified to route directly from the negative terminal (- / GND) of the main 24V power supply to the GND pin of the Arduino Mega 2560 microcontrollers. This design was required to establish a uniform zero-potential reference across all distinct power domains and eliminate common-mode voltage noise.

### Power Storage Specifications

For untethered operational profiles within theoretical battery decay models, the primary onboard energy storage was configured around a 6S Lithium Polymer (LiPo) Battery pack:

* **Capacity Recommendation:** 3000mAh – 5000mAh, balancing volumetric energy density against structural payload penalty.
* **Discharge Rate (C-Rating):** 30C or higher was required to support the instantaneous burst currents demanded by the 9 primary GIM6010-8 joint motors during sudden high-torque balancing maneuvers or rapid kinematic execution.

## Algorithmic Control and Discontinued Simulation Integration

Active tuning, policy training, and software pipeline integration have been permanently halted. The system's conceptualized real-time execution pipelines utilized a hierarchical reinforcement learning (RL) structure.

The control pipeline was designed to map human performance parameters directly onto the bionic robotic joint space. Human movement trajectories were processed into 55–60 skeletal keypoints formatted as real-time JSON payloads[^8]. These continuous geometric targets were decomposed into discrete, highly optimized movement primitives termed Micro-Operations[^7]. This architecture aimed to minimize computational overhead by mirroring Reduced Instruction Set Computing (RISC) principles[^7], allowing low-level distributed controllers to process trajectory chunks.

In virtual physics environments, the hierarchical RL controller was structured to blend a specialized dance/expression policy with an active balance safety policy[^8]. The system was designed to continuously sample stability margins by monitoring the Center of Mass (COM) and Center of Pressure (COP)[^8]. Under this design, if dynamic perturbations caused an unexpected shift:

$$\Delta \text{COP} > 0.03 \text{ m}$$

The hierarchical controller was designed to overrule the performance track within a $< 20 \text{ ms}$ control loop window[^8]. It modulated torque commands to the 9 GIM6010-8 joint motors to enforce active balance. No further policy deployment, training cycles, or sensor-fusion experiments will be executed.

## Conclusion and Project Closure

The Modified Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform V6 project is officially closed. Following the directive to stop all development across both physical fabrication and digital simulation domains, all engineering activities have ceased.
