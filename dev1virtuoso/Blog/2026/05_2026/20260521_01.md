# Transitioning Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform to the Community: Why am I Are Going Fully Open-Source

For the past ten years, the Heracles Project has been a journey of pushing the boundaries of humanoid robotics—exploring micro-operations, high-degree-of-freedom tendon-driven systems, and real-time reinforcement learning for expressive movement[^1][^2][^5]. Today, I am announcing a major strategic shift: I have decided to cease further investment in a physical, commercial production run of Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform. Instead, the entire project is being uploaded to GitHub under the MIT License, passing the torch to the global open-source community.

Here is an honest breakdown of why I made this decision and why going open-source is the best path forward for the legacy of Heracles.

## The Reality of Limited Personal Resources

Building a cutting-edge humanoid robot is an extraordinarily capital and labor-intensive endeavor. While earlier theoretical versions like V3 explored an uncompromising 141-motor architecture[^3][^4], and later versions like V5 brought build costs down to under HKD 6,000[^4], scaling the hardware to production demands resources beyond what an individual developer can sustainably provide. Managing procurement, precision manufacturing, assembly, and testing at scale requires institutional-level backing or deep corporate venture capital. Continuing alone as a personal project would restrict Heracles to a simulated environment or a single physical prototype, preventing it from reaching its full potential.

## Schedule Delays and the Loss of "First-Mover" Milestones

Innovation is a race against time. Due to persistent technical hurdles and sequential testing bottlenecks, its development timeline was severely extended and fell behind schedule. This delay had a critical consequence: it cost us its window to claim several foundational milestones that defined its market differentiation, including:
* **The first robot to perform K-Pop choreography**
* **The first robot to perform Canto-Pop choreography**
* **The first robot to execute a partnered duet dance**

Losing these definitive "world-firsts" drastically reduces its immediate competitive edge and commercial positioning. In technology, arriving with a brilliant design after the cultural window has shifted significantly diminishes the impact of a commercial launch.

## Rapid Market Convergence and Catch-up

Ten years ago, when the foundations of Heracles were laid, a high-DOF, underactuated, tendon-driven joint system inspired by bionic crustacean mechanics was highly futuristic[^6]. Even today, the design remains incredibly forward-looking and offers exceptional cost-to-performance advantages compared to traditional heavy-actuator designs[^6]. 

However, the robotics landscape has shifted dramatically. International research teams, commercial entities, and agile independent developers have caught up at an unprecedented pace. Cheap, low-cost humanoid platforms that can meet comparable performance requirements are rapidly entering the market. While Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform remains superior in mechanical flexibility and price-to-performance ratio, I can no longer guarantee an absolute, insurmountable competitive advantage in a commercial arena. 

## The Path Forward: Empowering the Community

Rather than allowing a ten-year design to sit idle or be choked by capital limitations, releasing it to the public ensures it becomes a living asset. The global maker, research, and educational communities can take this architecture and iterate on it faster than any single individual could. 

The Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform system[^4][^7] is now completely open. Anyone can pull the repository, build upon its reverse conjugate surface (RCS) geometries[^6], or adapt the hierarchical reinforcement learning control loop to their own hardware platforms[^7].

I will continue to provide non-commercial technical support to any team or individual working on fabricating or modifying Heracles Humanoid Robot Platform. I look forward to seeing how the community adapts this platform for education, makerspace projects, and expressive artistic performances.
